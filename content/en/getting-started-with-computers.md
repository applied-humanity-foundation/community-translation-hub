# Getting Started with Computers

## What is a Computer?

A computer is an electronic device that processes information according to a set of instructions called programs. Computers come in many forms — desktop computers, laptops, tablets, and smartphones are all types of computers.

At its core, every computer has these basic components:

- **Processor (CPU)**: The "brain" of the computer that performs calculations
- **Memory (RAM)**: Temporary storage for data being actively used
- **Storage**: Permanent storage for your files (hard drive or SSD)
- **Input devices**: Keyboard, mouse, touchscreen — how you tell the computer what to do
- **Output devices**: Monitor, speakers, printer — how the computer shows you results

## Turning On and Setting Up

When you first turn on a new computer, you'll go through a setup process:

1. Press the power button
2. Wait for the operating system to load
3. Follow the on-screen instructions to set your language, time zone, and create an account
4. Connect to the internet (Wi-Fi or ethernet cable)

Your **operating system** (like Windows, macOS, or Linux) is the software that manages everything on your computer. Think of it as the foundation that all other programs run on.

## Using a Mouse and Keyboard

The mouse is your pointer on screen:

- **Single click**: Select something
- **Double click**: Open something
- **Right click**: See more options
- **Scroll wheel**: Move up and down on a page

The keyboard has special keys you'll use often:

- **Enter**: Confirm an action
- **Backspace/Delete**: Remove text
- **Ctrl/Cmd + C**: Copy
- **Ctrl/Cmd + V**: Paste
- **Ctrl/Cmd + Z**: Undo your last action

## Files and Folders

Everything on your computer is organized into files and folders, just like a filing cabinet:

- **Files** are individual documents — a photo, a letter, a spreadsheet
- **Folders** are containers that hold files and other folders
- Your computer has a **file manager** (File Explorer on Windows, Finder on Mac) to browse everything

Good organization habits save time:

- Create folders for different categories (Work, Photos, School)
- Give files clear, descriptive names
- Regularly clean up files you no longer need

## Installing and Using Programs

Programs (also called applications or apps) let you do specific tasks:

- **Web browser** (Chrome, Firefox): Browse the internet
- **Word processor** (Word, Google Docs): Write documents
- **Spreadsheet** (Excel, Google Sheets): Work with numbers and data
- **Media player**: Watch videos or listen to music

To install a program, you can use your system's app store or download it from the program's official website. Always download software from trusted sources to stay safe.

## Getting Help

When something goes wrong:

1. Don't panic — most problems have simple solutions
2. Try restarting the computer (this fixes more issues than you'd expect)
3. Search for the error message online
4. Ask a friend, family member, or visit a local library for help
5. Many communities offer free digital literacy classes

Remember: everyone was a beginner once. The most important thing is to keep practicing and not be afraid to explore.
