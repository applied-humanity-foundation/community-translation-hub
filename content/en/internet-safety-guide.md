# Internet Safety Guide

## Why Internet Safety Matters

The internet is an incredible tool for learning, connecting, and creating. But just like the physical world, it has risks. Understanding these risks and how to protect yourself is essential for everyone who goes online.

## Protecting Your Accounts

### Strong Passwords

A strong password is your first line of defense:

- Use at least 12 characters
- Mix uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols
- Don't use personal information (birthday, pet's name)
- Use a different password for each account
- Consider using a **password manager** to keep track of them all

### Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security. Even if someone guesses your password, they can't get in without the second factor — usually a code sent to your phone or generated by an app.

Enable 2FA on all your important accounts: email, banking, social media.

## Recognizing Scams

### Phishing

Phishing is when someone pretends to be a trusted organization to trick you into giving them your information. Watch for:

- Emails that create urgency ("Your account will be closed!")
- Requests for passwords or personal information
- Suspicious sender addresses (look carefully — they may be slightly misspelled)
- Links that don't match the organization's real website

**Golden rule**: When in doubt, go directly to the website by typing the address yourself. Never click links in suspicious emails.

### Common Scam Types

- **Tech support scams**: Someone calls claiming your computer has a virus
- **Prize scams**: "You've won!" messages asking for payment to claim your prize
- **Romance scams**: Fake relationships designed to ask for money
- **Investment scams**: Promises of guaranteed high returns

## Privacy Online

### What You Share

Think before you post or share online:

- Personal details (full name, address, phone number) can be used for identity theft
- Photos may contain location data
- Information shared online can be very difficult to remove permanently
- Privacy settings on social media should be reviewed regularly

### Tracking and Cookies

Websites use cookies and trackers to monitor your activity:

- Use your browser's privacy settings to limit tracking
- Consider using a privacy-focused browser or extensions
- Regularly clear your cookies and browsing history
- Be cautious about accepting all cookies on every website

## Safe Browsing Habits

- Look for **HTTPS** (the padlock icon) in the address bar before entering sensitive information
- Keep your browser and operating system updated
- Don't download files from untrusted sources
- Be skeptical of ads that seem too good to be true
- Use antivirus software and keep it updated

## Protecting Children Online

If children in your life use the internet:

- Have open conversations about online safety
- Use parental controls appropriate for their age
- Teach them never to share personal information with strangers
- Monitor their online activity while respecting their growing independence
- Establish rules about screen time and appropriate content

## What to Do If Something Goes Wrong

- **Account hacked**: Change your password immediately, enable 2FA, check for unauthorized activity
- **Identity theft**: Contact your bank, file a report with local authorities, monitor your credit
- **Cyberbullying**: Save evidence, block the person, report to the platform, seek support
- **Scammed**: Report to your bank and local consumer protection agency

Remember: it's not your fault if you fall victim to a scam. Scammers are professionals. What matters is acting quickly to limit the damage.
