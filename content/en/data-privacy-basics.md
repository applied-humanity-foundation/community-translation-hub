# Data Privacy Basics

## What is Data Privacy?

Data privacy is about controlling who has access to your personal information and how it's used. In the digital age, companies, governments, and other organizations collect vast amounts of data about individuals — often without clear understanding or consent.

Your **personal data** includes:

- Name, address, phone number, email
- Financial information (bank accounts, credit cards)
- Health records
- Location history
- Online browsing habits
- Photos and messages
- Social media activity

## Why Data Privacy Matters

### Identity Theft

When personal information falls into the wrong hands, it can be used to:

- Open credit accounts in your name
- File fraudulent tax returns
- Access your bank accounts
- Impersonate you online

### Manipulation

Companies use your data to influence your behavior:

- Targeted advertising based on your habits
- Dynamic pricing (showing different prices to different people)
- Content algorithms that create filter bubbles
- Political campaigns targeting specific groups

### Discrimination

Data can be used to discriminate against people:

- Insurance companies raising rates based on health data
- Employers making decisions based on social media
- Landlords screening tenants using personal data
- Algorithms that perpetuate existing biases

## Your Rights

Many countries have passed data privacy laws that give you rights:

### Common Rights

- **Right to know**: What data is collected and how it's used
- **Right to access**: See the data a company has about you
- **Right to delete**: Request your data be removed
- **Right to opt out**: Say no to data collection or sharing
- **Right to correct**: Fix inaccurate data about you

### Key Privacy Laws

- **GDPR** (European Union): One of the strongest privacy laws globally
- **CCPA/CPRA** (California, USA): Gives California residents data rights
- **PIPL** (China): China's personal information protection law
- **LGPD** (Brazil): Brazil's general data protection law

## Protecting Your Privacy

### Online Habits

- Read privacy policies (or at least the summary) before signing up for services
- Use strong, unique passwords for each account
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Limit what you share on social media
- Use privacy-focused search engines and browsers
- Review app permissions on your phone regularly

### On Your Devices

- Keep your operating system and apps updated
- Use encrypted messaging apps for sensitive conversations
- Enable device encryption
- Set up a screen lock (PIN, password, fingerprint, or face ID)
- Be cautious with public Wi-Fi — use a VPN when possible

### Managing Your Digital Footprint

- Search for yourself online to see what's publicly available
- Request removal of unwanted information from search engines
- Close accounts you no longer use
- Be mindful of what you post — the internet has a long memory

## Data Privacy for Organizations

If you work with an organization that collects data:

- Only collect data you actually need
- Be transparent about how you use data
- Store data securely with encryption
- Have a clear privacy policy in plain language
- Respond promptly to data access and deletion requests
- Train all staff on data privacy practices

## Teaching Others

Help spread data privacy awareness:

- Talk to family members about privacy settings
- Help elderly relatives understand online scams
- Teach children about sharing information safely
- Support organizations working on digital rights
- Advocate for stronger privacy protections in your community
